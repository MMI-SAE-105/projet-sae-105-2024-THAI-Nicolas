The Time Machine by H.G. Wells (1895) is a foundational science fiction novel. Through the Time Traveler, it questions our relationship with time and the evolution of society, resonating with our contemporary concerns about technology.

Journey to the Heart of Time and Humanity

by Nicolas THAI

Published on 22/12 at 18:03

Illustration of time travel

I/ A Time Traveler Lost in the Ages

The novel follows the Time Traveler, creator of a machine to explore the future. He discovers a transformed Earth, populated by the Eloi and the Morlocks, symbolizing the consequences of human choices, like the prehistoric vacuum robot, a reflection of lost technology.

The Time Traveler, like a wandering robot, finds himself confronted with a world that has become foreign to him. His quest to understand these new species and their way of life resonates with the fundamental need of any form of intelligence, whether human or artificial, to find its place in a constantly changing environment.

II/ Misunderstanding and Confrontation with the Unknown

The Time Machine goes far beyond an adventure story, addressing misunderstanding and isolation. In meeting the Eloi, the Traveler discovers a weakened humanity, dependent on technology it no longer controls.

This evokes the prehistoric vacuum robot, wandering aimlessly in a world it does not understand. The indifference of the Eloi towards the Morlocks, who exploit them, raises questions about social inequalities and dehumanization, issues still relevant in a society where technology can both liberate and enslave.

III/ Reflection on Humanity and its Future

Through the Time Traveler, Wells invites us to reflect on our evolution and our relationship with technology. The wandering of the prehistoric vacuum robot resonates with that of the Traveler, confronted with a humanity deformed by its past. Both seek their place in a world that surpasses them. This quest for meaning, marked by solitude and existential anguish, transcends time. Like Wall-E, the Traveler embodies a universal struggle for understanding and acceptance, powerfully revealing the human condition.

The Time Machine goes beyond the realm of science fiction: it is a reflection on the human condition and the impact of technology. By exploring the interactions between human species, Wells pushes us to think about the future of our society and the consequences of our choices. Like the prehistoric vacuum robot, lost in a world that eludes it, we navigate through time and space in search of meaning and identity.
